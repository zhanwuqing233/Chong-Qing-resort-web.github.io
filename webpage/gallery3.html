Chongqing China Three Gorges Museum 重庆三峡博物馆

Description

Chongqing China Three Gorges Museum, also known as Chongqing Museum, is one of the first national first-class museums and national museums jointly built by the central and local governments. It is located in Yuzhong District, Chongqing, opposite Chongqing People's Great Hall. Its predecessor was Southwest Museum established in March 1951, renamed Chongqing Museum in June 1955, approved by The General Office of the State Council in September 2000, and added the name of "Chongqing Museum", its new museum was officially opened to the public on June 18, 2005. According to the official website of the museum in March 2016, the China Three Gorges Museum in Chongqing covers an area of 30,000 square meters, with a construction area of 45098 square meters and an exhibition area of 20,858 square meters. The exhibition consists of 4 basic exhibitions, 6 special exhibitions, 1 360-degree full-week film, 1 half-scene painting exhibition, 1 audience practice center and 3 temporary exhibitions. On November 18, 2020, it was elected as the "New Landmark of Bashu Cultural Tourism Corridor". In November 2022, it was selected into the supplementary recognition list of the first batch of national science popularization education bases from 2021 to 2025. In January 2023, it was selected into the list of the top 100 most-searched museums in China in 2022. In May 2023, it was listed by the Ministry of Culture and Tourism as one of 10 Yangtze River theme national tourism routes and Yangtze River Civilization Tracing Tour.
