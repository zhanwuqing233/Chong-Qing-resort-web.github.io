Liberation Monument 解放碑

Description

The Monument to the Victory of the Anti-Japanese War and the People's Liberation Monument, referred to as "Liberation Monument", is located in the center of the Jiefangbei commercial Pedestrian Street, Yuzhong District, Chongqing, People's Republic of China. It was started on October 31, 1946 and completed in August 1947. It is the spiritual symbol of the victory of the Anti-Japanese War and the only monument in China to commemorate the victory of the Chinese people's War of Resistance against Japanese Aggression. The monument of the Victory of the War of Resistance and the Monument of the People's Liberation is facing north to east. It is an eight-sided column with a reinforced concrete structure with a height of 27.5 meters and a side length of 2.55 meters. There are eight underground layers in the monument, a spiral ladder is installed at the top of the monument, and the four sides of the monument toward the street entrance are equipped with self-chimes. The place is a symbol of the victory of the Chinese people's anti-fascist war, as well as a symbol of the liberation of Chongqing and Chongqing. In September 2000, it was announced by Chongqing Municipal People's Government as the first batch of municipal cultural relics protection units. In May 2013, it was included in the "seventh batch of National Key cultural relics Protection Units" by The State Council of the People's Republic of China. On May 18, 2016, Jiefang Monument was selected as one of the "Top Ten Cultural Symbols of Chongqing". In September 2016, it was selected into the list of "the first batch of Chinese 20th Century Architectural Heritage". On November 18, 2020, it was selected as the "Top Ten New Landmarks of Chengdu-Chongqing Cultural Tourism".
